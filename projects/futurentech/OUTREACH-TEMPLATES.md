## Platform-Specific Notes:

**Reddit:**
- Be active in communities first (don't just spam)
- Engage in comments
- Share genuinely helpful insights
- Self-promotion should be <10% of activity

**Instagram:**
- Engage with posts before DMing
- Reference specific content
- Keep it personal, not template-y
- Use Stories for ongoing promotion

**Facebook Groups:**
- Check group rules first
- Engage in discussions
- Offer value before asking
- Don't cross-post same message to multiple groups

**Bandcamp:**
- Message artists whose work you genuinely enjoy
- Reference specific tracks/albums
- Keep it personal
- Respect if they don't respond

---

## Weekly Outreach Schedule:

**Monday:**
- Search Reddit for new artists (r/postrock, r/indieheads, genre subs)
- Engage in discussions, share channel where relevant

**Wednesday:**
- Bandcamp discovery (use tags: DIY, indie, your favorite genres)
- Message 5-10 artists

**Friday:**
- Instagram hashtag search (#DIYmusician, #indieartist, genre tags)
- Engage + DM promising artists

**Daily:**
- Respond to form submissions
- Engage with artists who engage with channel
- Share content, build relationships

---

## Success Metrics:

Track:
- Form submissions per week
- Response rate to outreach
- Conversion from submission to feature
- Quality of submissions (engagement, music quality, strategy interest)

Goal: 10-20 quality submissions per week within first month

---

## DO's and DON'Ts:

**DO:**
- Personalize every outreach
- Reference specific music/content
- Be genuine about what you like
- Respect "no" or no response
- Follow up once, then move on
- Build relationships, not just transactions

**DON'T:**
- Spam
- Use generic copy-paste messages
- Reach out to artists you haven't listened to
- Over-promise results
- Ask for payment or quid pro quo
- Be pushy

---

**Remember:** You're offering value (exposure + strategy analysis). Be confident but respectful. Not every artist will be interested, and that's fine. Focus on those who are excited about the concept.
