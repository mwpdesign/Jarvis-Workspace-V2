## ‚öôÔ∏è KLING AI SETTINGS GUIDE

### **Creativity Slider**:
- **60-70%**: Safe, enhances without changing too much (most shots)
- **75-80%**: Adds cinematic flair, more atmosphere (emotional beats)
- **85%+**: Maximum enhancement (bottle smash, key metaphors)

### **Motion Level**:
- **Low**: Static shots, close-ups, tripod shots
- **Medium**: Performance, handheld, natural movement
- **High**: Avoid (too much warping for music video)

### **Other Toggles**:
- **Enhance Lighting**: ON for all warehouse/attic shots
- **Add Atmosphere**: ON for moody/dramatic scenes
- **Maximum Detail**: ON for close-ups (eyes, reactions, bottle)

## üé¨ FINAL CUT PRO INTEGRATION

### **Thursday Morning: Import Kling AI Clips**

**Step 1**: Create new Library in Final Cut
- Name: `Kling-AI-Enhanced`
- Import all 39 processed clips

**Step 2**: Compare Original vs Kling AI
- Use **Comparison Viewer** (Command + Y)
- A/B test each shot
- Verify quality, no artifacts

**Step 3**: Replace on Timeline
- Drag Kling AI version over original on timeline
- Or use **Replace Clip** function
- Keep both available for flexibility

**Step 4**: Color Matching
- Create LUT from Kling AI output (use Color Board)
- Apply to original ProRes shots that didn't get Kling treatment
- Ensures visual consistency across all 6 reels

## üö® TROUBLESHOOTING

### **Problem**: Kling AI output has artifacts/warping
**Solution**: 
- Lower creativity setting (try 60-65%)
- Simplify prompt (less descriptive)
- Use original ProRes instead

### **Problem**: Processing taking forever (>2 hours)
**Solution**:
- Check Kling AI server status
- May be high traffic - try different time
- Longer clips take longer

### **Problem**: Colors don't match original
**Solution**:
- That's OK! Kling adds cinematic color
- Color grade originals in Final Cut to match
- Creates cohesive film aesthetic

### **Problem**: Running out of credits
**Solution**:
- Prioritize maximum priority shots only
- Skip medium-priority shots
- Buy more credits (if budget allows)
